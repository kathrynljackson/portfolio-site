About Me

from teacher to software developer

My career began in education. I worked for six years as a teacher where I taught students of many ability levels and socio-economic backgrounds. After earning the position of the school Instructional Technology Implementation Representative I was afforded many opportunities such as being invited to speak two years in a row at the West Georgia STEM conference, where I gave presentations on how to use technology in early elementary classrooms in an age appropriate and logical way. Here, I was able to help fellow educators, learn new skills, and make professional connections.

I was accepted into Turing School of Software and Design in Denver, CO where I served as a Student Leadership Committee Representative, requiring me to function as a liaison between students and administration, facilitate retros, and ensure that my classmates had access to the resources needed to be successful. While here, I worked with several frameworks including React, React Native, ES6 and Redux. The project-based curriculum at the school allowed me to explore areas that are often seen as backend concepts,such as building a database using Express, Ruby on Rails, and Postgres.

My objective as a programmer is to make technology more accessible for all.
